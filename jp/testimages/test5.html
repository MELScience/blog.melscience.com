When light hits an iron ion Fe³+  from ammonium iron(III) citrate, it initiates a reaction in which Fe³+  gains an electron  , turning (or, as chemists say, reducing) into Fe²+ . But the product of this reaction is not yet visible, and that’s where potassium hexacyanoferrate(III)  comes into play. It does not stick to Fe³+ , but once it meets Fe²+ , it forms an insoluble intensely-colored compound called Prussian blue. This is what we see when we look at a cyanotype image.
